2 Chapter 1: Anatomical language

2.1 Your objectives for this lab are to:

  • Define and demonstrate the anatomical position.
  • Use the correct terminology to describe the planes of the body.
  • Identify the planes of view is several different views of the body.
  • Define and demonstrate the different anatomical positions.
  • Define and demonstrate the directional terms used in anatomy.
  • Define and apply the different anatomical regions.
  • Locate the major cavities and describe several of the organs that lie within each.
  • Identify the abdominopelvic cavities’:
  • Four quadrants and their organs
  • Nine regions and their organs
  • Identify the serous membranes within the body and their respective layers.

## Lab Activity 1.5

2.23 Applying Anatomical and Regional Terminology

Starting Position Instructions Given to Move to Anatomical Position
1: From lying face-up on the ground with their head, back, hands, and feet on the floor with both knees bent (Figure 1.7) • Extend both knees inferiorly until the lower limbs are straight.
• Position the upper limbs at the sides of the trunk.
• Rotate the palmar regions anteriorly.
• Position the head and torso facing anteriorly.
2: From a seated position on the floor with their legs straight and arms folded across their chest (Figure 1.8) • Extend the torso superiorly into a standing position.
• Position the upper limbs at the sides of the trunk.
• Rotate the palmar regions anteriorly.
• Position the lower limbs together and facing anteriorly.
• Position the head facing anteriorly.

2.24 Sectioning/Body Planes

2.24.1 Body Planes

  • Sagittal plane – divides the body into left and right portions

  • Midsagittal plane – divides the body into equal left and right halves

  • Parasagittal plane – divides the body into unequal left and right portions

  • Transverse plane – divides the body into superior and inferior portions

  • Coronal plane – divides the body into anterior and posterior portions

  • Oblique plane – diagonal cut through the body or limb (draw diagonally across the right brachial region)

3.5 Chapter 1 – Anatomical Language Glossary (Simplified)

  • Abdominal – relating to the abdomen

  • Abdominal cavity – contains digestive organs

  • Abdominopelvic cavity – contains abdominal + pelvic organs

  • Anabolism – builds complex molecules

  • Anatomical position – standard body position for reference

  • Anatomy – study of body structure

  • Anterior (ventral) – front

  • Posterior (dorsal) – back

  • Superior (cranial) – above

  • Inferior (caudal) – below

  • Medial – toward midline

  • Lateral – away from midline

  • Proximal – closer to attachment

  • Distal – farther from attachment

  • Superficial – near surface

  • Deep – away from surface

  • Cranial cavity – contains brain

  • Spinal cavity – contains spinal cord

  • Thoracic cavity – contains heart + lungs

  • Abdominopelvic cavity – contains digestive + pelvic organs

  • Frontal plane – divides front/back

  • Sagittal plane – divides left/right

  • Transverse plane – divides top/bottom

  • Homeostasis – stable internal environment

  • Negative feedback – returns to normal

  • Positive feedback – amplifies change

  • Cell – smallest living unit

  • Tissue – group of similar cells

  • Organ – group of tissues

  • Organ system – group of organs

  • Organism – living being

  • Nervous system – control + communication

  • Endocrine system – hormones

  • Cardiovascular system – transport

  • Respiratory system – gas exchange

  • Digestive system – breaks down food

  • Muscular system – movement

  • Skeletal system – support

  • Integumentary system – protection

  • Urinary system – waste removal

  • Reproductive system – reproduction

  • Lymphatic system – immunity

  • Serous membrane – reduces friction

  • Pleura – lungs

  • Pericardium – heart

  • Peritoneum – abdominal organs

  • Prone – face down

  • Supine – face up
